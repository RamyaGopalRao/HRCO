Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Policy

1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to provide a safe and respectful work environment free from sexual harassment, and to outline the procedures for addressing complaints of sexual harassment.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, trainees, interns, and any other individuals associated with the company.

3. Definition of Sexual Harassment
Sexual harassment includes, but is not limited to, the following behaviors:

Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.

Creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment through inappropriate comments, jokes, gestures, or actions.

Any form of unwelcome conduct that has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an individual's work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

4. Examples of Sexual Harassment
Physical conduct such as unwanted touching, patting, pinching, or brushing against another person.

Verbal conduct such as making sexual comments, jokes, or gestures, or using sexually explicit language.

Non-verbal conduct such as displaying sexually suggestive objects, pictures, or gestures.

Making employment decisions based on submission to or rejection of sexual advances.

5. Responsibilities
Employees: Employees are responsible for maintaining a respectful work environment and reporting any incidents of sexual harassment.

Supervisors and Managers: Supervisors and managers must take prompt and appropriate action to address complaints of sexual harassment and ensure compliance with this policy.

HR Department: The HR department is responsible for conducting investigations, providing training, and ensuring that the policy is enforced.

6. Complaint Procedure
Reporting: Any employee who experiences or witnesses sexual harassment should report the incident to their supervisor, manager, or the HR department as soon as possible.

Confidentiality: All complaints will be treated with the utmost confidentiality, and information will only be shared on a need-to-know basis.

Investigation: The HR department will conduct a thorough and impartial investigation of the complaint, including interviews with relevant parties and review of any evidence.

Action: If the investigation finds that sexual harassment has occurred, appropriate disciplinary action will be taken against the offender, which may include termination of employment.

7. False Complaints
Deliberately making false or malicious complaints of sexual harassment is strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action.

8. Training and Awareness
The company will provide regular training and awareness programs on the prevention of sexual harassment to all employees.

9. Support for Victims
The company will provide support to victims of sexual harassment, including counseling services and assistance in accessing external resources if needed.

10. Contact Information
For any questions or concerns regarding this policy, please contact the HR department at hr@company.com.
